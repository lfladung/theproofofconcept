Meta Progression & Gear Evolution Design

Core Philosophy

Flat stats make you viable. Systems make you powerful.

The refined progression model has two clean contexts:

- In runs: Infusions define temporary power and combat expression.
- Between runs: Gear upgrades and gems define permanent progression.

Gear progression should not be something the player manages during a run. Runs
create pressure, rewards, and proof of mastery; the hub is where gear changes.

Player Mental Model

During a run, I shape my power through Infusions.

After the run, I upgrade my gear tier and tune it with gems.

System Hierarchy

Layer	System	Role
In-Run Power	Infusions	Temporary build-shaping, pillar fantasy, and live combat expression
Core Meta	Gear Tiers + Promotion	Permanent identity, mastery, and long-term progression
Build Expression	Gems	Between-run customization layered onto gear
Economy	Pillar Materials	Funds tier upgrades and gem crafting/refreshing
Passive	Familiarity	Smooths long-term gear attachment in the background

Removed Or Reframed Systems

Tempering is removed as a player-facing gear progression concept.

Attunement and Inscriptions are also removed as standalone systems.

Their useful jobs are merged into:

- Infusions: in-run power and pillar expression
- Gear Tiers: permanent identity and specialization
- Gems: flexible customization and experimentation

This avoids asking players to understand whether a build comes from gear,
infusions, tempering, attunement, inscriptions, or gems. The answer should be:

- This run power is my Infusion.
- This permanent identity is my Gear Tier.
- These flexible modifiers are my Gems.

1. In-Run Power: Infusions

Concept

Infusions are the only in-run build-shaping system.

They already carry the game's live pillar fantasy through thresholds such as:

- Baseline
- Escalated
- Expression

The exact names can vary by pillar, but the structure is consistent: a run starts
with a readable pillar effect, escalates through play, and can reach a high-expression
state that changes the feel of combat.

Infusions Own

- Temporary in-run power
- Live pillar identity
- Combat feedback
- Resonance spending and reroll pressure
- End-of-run conversion into materials

Infusions Do Not Own

- Permanent gear upgrades
- Gear tier unlocks directly in the field
- Socket management
- Inventory sorting

Design Rules

- Infusions are run-scoped.
- Infusions can be powerful and expressive because they reset.
- Infusions should have clear HUD/VFX feedback.
- Infusions can influence rewards, but they do not mutate gear during the run.

Example: Edge Run Power

Baseline:

- Crit or precision identity comes online.

Escalated:

- The crit loop becomes more active or conditional.

Expression:

- The run reaches the strongest Edge fantasy for this attempt.

2. Core Meta: Gear Tier Progression

Structure

Each piece of gear evolves through 3 permanent tiers:

Tier 1 - Base

- Neutral identity
- Basic functionality
- Minimal stats
- Limited interactions

Tier 2 - Aligned

- Commits to a pillar such as Edge, Flow, Mass, Echo, Anchor, Phase, or Surge
- Unlocks the gear's core permanent behavior
- Gives a moderate stat increase
- Makes the item feel meaningfully different from its base version

Tier 3 - Specialized

- Fully expresses the pillar concept
- Adds new mechanics or rule-bending behavior
- Gives the final capped stat increase
- Turns the item into a clear long-term build identity

Design Rules

- Evolution is irreversible unless a later respec system is deliberately added.
- Upgrade outcomes are intentional, not random.
- Each path should be qualitatively different, not just numerically stronger.
- Gear changes happen outside the run.
- Gear tier defines the player's long-term build identity.

Example: Sword

Tier 1 - Basic Blade

- Standard melee
- No specialization

Tier 2 - Edge Blade

- Crit-focused behavior
- Overkill spill

Tier 3 - Execution Blade

- Execute thresholds
- Primed target amplification

3. Promotion

Concept

Promotion converts run accomplishments into eligibility for permanent gear
progression.

Promotion is not a mid-run transformation. It is the bridge between run mastery
and post-run upgrades.

How It Works

During a run, players complete meaningful objectives:

- Clear floors
- Defeat bosses
- Complete challenges
- Reach strong Infusion states
- Finish missions with pillar-specific accomplishments

This grants Promotion Progress toward a gear tier upgrade.

Result

When Promotion Progress and material costs are met, the player can upgrade gear
between runs.

Design Impact

- Progression is tied to skill and execution.
- Deep runs matter.
- The run stays focused on Infusions.
- The hub/loadout phase stays focused on gear upgrades.

4. Build Expression: Gems

Concept

Gems are the only flexible between-run build customization system.

They modify behavior, reinforce pillar identity, and create experimentation.
They do not compete with attunement or inscriptions because those no longer exist
as separate systems.

Gems Own

- Customization
- Build diversity
- Experimentation
- Small behavior twists on top of gear identity

Examples

- Edge: crits apply bleed
- Flow: chaining extends dodge windows
- Surge: charged attacks slow enemies
- Echo: a triggered effect has a chance to repeat
- Mass: impact effects add extra stagger

Recommended Durability Model: Fatigue

Gems weaken over repeated use and eventually require refreshing or replacement.

Purpose

- Encourages experimentation
- Prevents solved loadouts
- Keeps runs feeding back into build decisions
- Adds pressure without loot clutter

5. Economy: Pillar Materials

Concept

Materials are directional progression resources.

Clarity Rule

Materials push you deeper into a pillar.

Primary Resource: Pillar Materials

Runs produce pillar materials from completed objectives, held infusions, boss
rewards, and mission outcomes.

Examples:

- Edge material
- Flow material
- Surge material
- Mass material
- Echo material
- Anchor material
- Phase material

Materials Only Do

- Pay Promotion costs for tier upgrades
- Craft gems
- Refresh or repair fatigued gems

Removed Uses

Materials no longer fund separate attunement trees, inscription tracks, or other
layered upgrade systems.

Secondary Resource: Resonant Dust

Optional.

Resonant Dust can exist as a small universal pacing resource, but it should not
become the main decision currency.

6. Passive Progression: Familiarity

Concept

Familiarity is background gear XP.

It should be supportive, subtle, and mostly invisible.

Rules

- No major decision-making tied to it
- No build-defining behavior
- No separate upgrade tree
- Small capped bonuses only

Purpose

- Smooths progression feel
- Rewards using a favorite item
- Helps long-term gear feel personal without adding another system to manage

Example Bonus Targets

Level	Bonus
Familiar	+2%
Seasoned	+4%
Masterwork	+6%

7. Stat Progression

Purpose

Stats smooth the difficulty curve. They do not define the build.

Rules

- Stats are small
- Stats are capped
- Stats are non-dominant
- Total growth should stay bounded

Sources

- Gear Tiers
- Small familiarity bonuses
- Gems, when a gem deliberately has a stat-facing effect

Target Scaling

Aim for roughly 1.5x-2x total growth from early game to endgame.

8. Difficulty Scaling

Rule

Difficulty increases through complexity, not stat inflation.

Methods

- Enemy composition
- Multi-family encounters
- Synergy-based threats
- Smarter targeting
- Increased density
- Cross-pillar enemy hybridization

Avoid

- HP inflation
- Pure damage scaling
- Progression that requires number grinding instead of mastery

9. Full Progression Loop

During Run

- Fight enemies
- Gain and shape Infusions
- Spend resonance on in-run infusion decisions
- Complete objectives
- Earn rewards, Promotion Progress, and materials

Post Run Rewards

- Pillar materials
- Gems or gem crafting inputs
- Optional Resonant Dust
- Promotion Progress

Between Runs

Player chooses to:

- Upgrade gear tiers
- Craft or refresh gems
- Socket gems
- Swap gear from the small arsenal

Long-Term Outcome

- Gear becomes personalized
- Builds become intentional
- Runs become expressive, not inventory-heavy

Final Model Summary

In Runs

- Infusions only

Permanent Progression

- Gear Tiers
- Promotion Progress
- Small familiarity smoothing

Customization

- Gems only

Economy

- Pillar Materials
- Optional Resonant Dust

Core Insight

One system defines the run. One system defines the gear. One system modifies the gear.

Defines the run: Infusions

Defines the gear: Gear Tier

Modifies the gear: Gems
