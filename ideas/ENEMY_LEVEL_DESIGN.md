🔷 Core Principle: “Introduce → Reinforce → Twist → Master”

I want a learning curve model.

Each layer should follow this pattern:

1. Introduce (safe exposure)
New enemy behavior appears in isolation or low pressure
Player can observe, not just react

2. Reinforce (repeat with variation)
Same enemy appears in mixed groups
Player starts forming strategy

3. Twist (force adaptation)
Enemy appears in new context (pairings, room layouts)
This is where difficulty spikes meaningfully

4. Master (test)
Player must handle multiple mechanics simultaneously
Boss or elite encounter validates understanding

🧠 Suggested Structure (Cleaner + More Intentional)

Instead of loosely scaling counts, define each floor’s purpose:

👉 Important:

This layer should feel like:

“Oh, I thought I understood this system…”

🔥 Layer 3 — Deep + System Mastery

Now you’re right: just “more deep enemies” isn’t enough.

You need new dimensions of difficulty, not just stronger enemies.

💡 Layer 3 Difficulty Ideas (This is where your design shines)
1. Hybridization (your idea — VERY strong)

Enemies inherit traits across families:

Edge + Flow → fast, precise dash attackers
Mass + Anchor → immovable control zones
Echo + Surge → duplicating burst units

👉 Important rule:

Keep hybrids visually readable
Always 1 dominant trait, 1 secondary
2. Room Modifiers (low cost, high impact)

Instead of only changing enemies, change rules of the space:

“Projectiles phase through walls” (Phase influence)
“Enemies gain momentum over time” (Flow)
“Repeated hits echo” (Echo)

👉 This ties directly into your infusion pillars = huge cohesion win

3. Enemy Roles (instead of just tiers)

At deep layer, enemies shouldn’t just be “stronger”—they should have roles:

Pressure (forces movement)
Punish (kills mistakes)
Control (limits space)
Support (buffs others)

👉 Difficulty becomes:

“Can the player solve the composition?”

4. Composition Design (THIS is the real endgame)

Instead of random mixes, define encounter archetypes:

“Sniper + Pusher”
“Spawner + Tank”
“Glass Cannon swarm”

This is where your system becomes designed, not procedural chaos.
