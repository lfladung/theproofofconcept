Enemy Family #1: Threadline / Pattern Creatures

Core Idea: Movement as a “drawn path”

Deep Version: Pattern Devourer

A creature that moves like it’s drawing reality behind it.

Behavior:
Glides in smooth, geometric curves
Leaves persistent damaging trails
Anticipates player movement (cuts off paths)
Can “complete shapes” to trigger effects:
circle → AoE burst
line → slicing beam
Combat Role:
Area denial
Forces repositioning
Visual:
Clean, flowing, almost too perfect
Glowing path behind it like a neon ribbon

Mid Version: Weave Serpent

It’s learning to move properly

Behavior:
Tracks players with slight delay
Leaves short-lived trails
Occasionally overshoots targets
Combat Feel:

Teaches players:

“Don’t stand where it will be”

Surface Version: Threadling

Barely functioning

Behavior:
Moves in straight, jittery lines
Frequently pauses or stutters
Trail flickers and often disappears
Combat Feel:
Easy, but weirdly unpredictable
Design Purpose
Teaches movement prediction early
Pays off later with area control mastery

Enemy Family #2: Impact / Mass Creatures

Core Idea: Weight, force, and inevitability

Deep Version: Gravity Colossus

A walking distortion of weight

Behavior:
Slow but pulls players inward
Slams create shockwaves + gravity wells
Projectiles curve toward it
Combat Role:
Battlefield controller
Forces spacing discipline
Visual:
Dense, heavy, chunks floating around it
Ground cracks under its steps

Mid Version: Stone Ram

Functional but limited

Behavior:
Charges in straight lines
Knocks players back
Gets briefly stuck after impact
Combat Feel:

Teaches:

“Respect momentum”

Surface Version: Lumpback

Incomplete weight

Behavior:
Slow waddling
Occasional clumsy slam
Often misses entirely
Combat Feel:
Feels harmless… until it connects
 Design Purpose
Introduces positioning + spacing
Later becomes high-pressure zone control

Enemy Family #3: Echo / Replication Creatures

Core Idea: Repetition, duplication, cascading effects

Deep Version: Reverberant Host

One action becomes many

Behavior:
Every attack repeats with delay
Creates afterimages that act independently
Can “store” attacks and release them later
Combat Role:
Chaos generator
Forces awareness of time + space
Visual:
Multiple overlapping silhouettes
Actions echo visually before occurring

Mid Version: Twin Shade

Learning duplication

Behavior:
Splits into 2 briefly
Second attack follows shortly after first
Combat Feel:

Teaches:

“Don’t react once—react twice”

Surface Version: Flicker

Unstable repetition

Behavior:
Occasionally “double moves”
Attacks sometimes repeat unintentionally
Combat Feel:
Feels glitchy, not threatening yet
